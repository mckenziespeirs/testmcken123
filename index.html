What is a Genre?

The official definition is a category of artistic composition, as in music or literature, characterized by similarities in form, style, or subject matter. For films, they can be put into different genres based on their tone, theme/topic, mood, format, target audience, budget, etc. There are many different movie genres, but I will only be talking about three major ones: action, romance, and horror.

Genre 1: Action

Action is a very popular genre of movie. Action film is a film genre in which the protagonist is thrust into a series of events that typically involve violence and physical feats. There are sub-genres underneath this as well, such as westerns action, war and military, spy, martial arts, sci-fi action, superhero action and disaster, action. Obviously there are many more sub-genres of the action genre, and you would get a different answer depending on who you asked, but these are the sub-genres most commonly displayed.

Genre 2: Romance

Romance is another popular genre when it comes to movies. Romance movies usually have a plot that revolves around the love between two protagonists. This genre usually has a theme that explores an issue within love, including but not limited to: love at first sight, forbidden love, love triangles, and sacrificial love. The romance genre is a very vast one, and the subgenres that fall under it are numerous, but some exaples are historical romance, regency romance, romantic drama, romanitc comedy, paranormal romance, etc.
